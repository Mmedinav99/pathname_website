  • What does a Koala bear look like?

    Koalas have soft, wool-like fur that is gray above and white below...

  • What is the endangered status of the Koalas?

    Koala bears are classified as vulnerable to be endangered.

  • How large can Koalas get?

    They weigh about 30 pounds and grow to be around 2 feet tall.

  • What is the population of Koalas?

    There are fewer than 100,000 koalas.

  • What is the average lifespan of a koala?

    Koalas can live up to 17 years, though males often live only 2–10 years.

  • Where do Koalas live?

    Today they are found in Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, and South Australia.

  • What type of environment do Koalas like?

    Koalas prefer eucalyptus forests, coastal islands, and low woodlands.

  • What do Koalas eat?

    They eat eucalyptus leaves and bark from 12 tree species, plus mistletoe and box leaves.

  • How do Koalas behave?

    They are nocturnal and arboreal, sleeping up to 16 hours a day and living alone in trees.

  • How do Koalas populate?

    Koalas breed yearly. Babies live in the pouch 5–7 months before becoming independent.

  • What are some major threats to Koalas?

    Habitat loss, traffic deaths, and dog attacks kill ~4,000 koalas annually.

  • How can we protect Koalas?

    Australian laws protect koalas, but states manage their conservation directly.
